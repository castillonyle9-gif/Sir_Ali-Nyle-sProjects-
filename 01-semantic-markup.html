The Art of the Pour Over

Published on 14 February 2026

The pour over method is one of the most precise and rewarding ways to brew coffee at home. It gives you full control over every variable — water temperature, pour rate, and grind size.

Quick Tip

Use water that is 93–96°C for optimal extraction. Boiling water can over-extract and make your cup bitter.

Start with a medium-fine grind, pre-wet your filter, and pour in slow, controlled spirals. The total brew time should be around 3 minutes.

Understanding Coffee Origins

Published on 30 January 2026

Where a coffee is grown has an enormous impact on its flavour. Ethiopian coffees are famous for their floral and fruity notes, while Colombian beans tend to be balanced and nutty.

Top 3 Origins for Beginners

  1. Ethiopia — bright, fruity, complex
  2. Colombia — smooth, balanced, approachable
  3. Brazil — chocolatey, low acidity, easy drinking
